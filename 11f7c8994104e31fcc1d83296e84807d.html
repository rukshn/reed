    I'm going to talk about what I saw in BlockEvent a block chain based event platform. I first saw it when it started registering users for Dialog Google IO extended event.

    At that time I was sceptical why you need a block chain based event registration system when you can handle it in a usual centralized system. But the platform was new and it was yet to catch up. So I moved on and yester came to see their Facebook page for any new updates.

    Then it hit me. A Facebook user rating of 1.1. 1.1 is beyond bad. So I went to see what users are saying about the platform. Almost all of them are geeks who applied for the Google IO. So I'm sure they know what they are talking about.

    Block event made several mistakes I'm not sure how the applications are reviewed and how the participants are selected in BlockEvent because there is no centralized database and all the data is stored in the block. But for some reason they selected the wrong candidates

    Another mistake was for some reason they sent emails to candidates who did not get selected for the event.

    And at the same time some of the people who got selected forgot their passwords by the time they received the confirmation email. So they were locked out from logging in to the block event account. Because there is no central database they are unable to reset their passwords.

    The passwords are hashed and stored in the block. And once you put something in the block you undo it. Because one block is linked to another and so on. If you edit one block in the past that would make the next block invalid and the whole system collapses.

    Also if the emails are stored in the block how can they send emails confirming the participation? Are the email stored in plain text? Or are they keeping a centralized database of emails? How is the logging in process work?

    For every complaint the block event has the same response. That the system is decentralized and they have no option to reset the passwords. According to the following response no data is stored in a centralized system. Not even the user credentials.

    For something as simple as a ticketing and event management system do you need block chain technology?? Facebook, Eventbrite and other similar platforms are handling thousands of events and ticketing requests with a centralized system.

    So do you need a decentralized system for a single event with few hundreds of participants? What benefit does it do in contrast to a centralized system other than keeping your data decentralized?

    Luckily the event was cancelled and it will be a live stream. I hope BlockEvent can bounce back and learn from their mistakes. That is the only way to improve.

    I like to have your opinion as tech and block chain enthusiasts on using block chain technology on simple tasks. IMO block chain is good but it doesn't mean you should replace everything with block chain. @ishanmarikar @_manZzup_ @thameera

    About the thing about ticket fraud and black market sales I think a centralized system solve that as well. Each ticket can be attached to a social account like Facebook or Google. So they cannot be sold again or can do fraud. Because one ticket is attached to one social account

    When it comes to BlockEvent they could have prevented the password mixup by adding a confirmation field. They do not have a field to confirm the password. How many times did you have a typo in a password field?
